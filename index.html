About Research CV

Robert Jagiello

I am a researcher in social psychology and evolutionary anthropology interested in the motivations that guide belief formation, social learning and cultural transmission. I completed my PhD at the University of Oxford under the supervision of Prof Harvey Whitehouse, based at the Centre for the Study of Social Cohesion (CSSC). My doctoral research examined why some cultural practices and beliefs (such as rituals, conventions, norms and traditions) are transmitted with high fidelity and resist change, while others remain flexible and open to innovation.

I am equally interested in supernatural and conspiratorial belief systems, the social cognition underlying moral norms and the evolution of morality as a cooperative device. Across these domains, my work asks how beliefs that are weakly supported by direct evidence can nonetheless become socially compelling, morally charged and resistant to correction.

My work integrates experimental psychology, cultural evolutionary theory and computational approaches, with earlier training in cognitive neuroscience using EEG and pupillometry.
